Relationship Agreement

Auckland Regional Landfill (ARL)

Partnership with WM NZ & Ngāti Manuhiri, Nga Mauna Whakahii o Kaipara, Te uri o Hau, Ngati Whatua
Active Signed 19 August 2025
↗Context & Purpose

The ARL Consolidated Agreement is a binding, multi‑party agreement that establishes the long‑term framework governing the relationship between WM NZ and affected iwi in relation to the Auckland Regional Landfill at Wayby Valley. It replaces earlier, iwi‑specific arrangements with a single, coherent agreement intended to endure for the full lifecycle of the landfill.

The ARL project was developed to provide replacement landfill capacity for Auckland as existing sites near closure. The project prompted significant iwi concerns and Environment Court appeals. Waste Management initially resolved these issues through bilateral Heads of Agreement with individual iwi. The Consolidated Agreement was subsequently developed to unify those arrangements and provide enduring certainty post‑consent.

◈Principles
  • Engagement Principle 1 - Recognition of need for new regional landfill
    Both Parties recognise that a new Landfill is needed to service Auckland's residual waste.
  • Engagement Principle 2 - Recognition of Mana Whenua
    WM recognises iwi as holding authority and exercising responsibilities to their uri and to each other in accordance with tikanga.
  • Engagement Principle 3 - WM recognises Te Awa o Hoteo is a taonga and that Mana Whenua hold kaitiakitanga status
    WM to take reasonable steps to support and protect the Te Awa o Hooteo and acknowledge that without adequate management the Landfill could impact Te Awa o Hooteo.
  • Engagement Principle 4 - Ongoing strategic relationships between tangata whenua and WM
    Parties agree to work together on future sustainable waste technologies and solutions.
✦Outcomes & Commitments
  • Bond for Te Awa o Hoteo and Kaipara Moana: WM will make arrangements for a $XX bond that will provide assurance that taonga will be protected from adverse effects.
  • No leachate: Safeguard taonga from leachate contamination arising from Landfill operations. If any detection, and if not remedied within one year of detection, the Consent Holder will cease receiving all waste.
  • Contaminated Sites: The Consent Conditions include a commitment to accept up to 50,000 tonnes of waste to the ARL once operational free of charge.
  • Sediment: In the event the Landfill has a positive sediment balance, WM just develop a Sediment Balance Plan to mitigate this.
  • Offsets: WM will engage with iwi on a stream offset under Consent Conditions 120-121.
  • Collaboration Projects: WM will engage with tangata whenua parties on development of waste reduction and minimisation initiatives for the Auckland Region.
  • Communications & Media: WM recognises that any communications it wishes to make must be commnicated to the Chair or Deputy Chair or their authorised representative.

Relationship Agreement

Ngāti Manuhiri Heads of Agreement

Partnership with WM NZ
Active Signed 22 December 2022
↗Context & Purpose

The Head of Agreement was negotiated and executed in December 2022, at a point when the Auckland Regional Landfill (ARL) project was initiated. This is an agreement with Manuhiri where they sought recognition and meaningful mitigation for offsets for effects, protection and tangible outcomes.

At that time, WM required certainty to progress the project.

◈Principles
  • Engagement Principle 1 - Recognition of need for new regional landfill
    Both Parties recognise that a new Landfill is needed to service Auckland's residual waste.
  • Engagement Principle 2 - Recognition of Mana Whenua
    WM recognises iwi as holding authority and exercising responsibilities to their uri and to each other in accordance with tikanga.
  • Engagement Principle 3 - Ongoing strategic relationships between tangata whenua and WM
    Parties agree to work together on future sustainable waste technologies and solutions.
  • Engagement Principle 4 - ARL Engagement
    Ngāti Manuhiri will be engaged on the day-to-day operations of the ARL.
  • Engagement Principle 5 - Iwi Stakeholder Engagement
  • Engagement Principle 6 - Restoration of Site and Ngāti Manuhiri rohe.
✦Outcomes & Commitments
  • Work Programme: Parties will work together to develop a work programme to provide employment opportunities throughout WM's operation across Aotearoa.
  • Financial Contribution: WM will make a payment or payments of up to $2 million excl GST to Ngāti Manuhiri in relation to the actual or reasonable construction of up to six dwellings.
  • Business Opportunities: Parties agree a set of guidelines used to investigate business opportunities that Parties are committed to investigating them and if appropriate, progressing them.
  • Future Waste Technology and Infrastructure Co-Development: Ngāti Manuhiri will lead and WM will assist, in jointly developing future waste disposal technologies and infrastructure within Ngāti Manuhiri's rohe.
  • Protection of Native Flora, Fauna, and Water Quality: WM commits to protecting taonga on the site.
⇄Engagement Structure
Co-chaired Tangata Whenua Executive Committee - Bi-annual
Community hui - Annual
↻Review & Termination
  • Heads of Agreement does not specify a formal review cycle or review frequency. The HoA relies on ongoing engagement structures to surface and address issues over time.

Relationship Agreement

Ngāti Kuku

Partnership with WM NZ & Ngāti Kuku
Active Signed 12 November 2025
↗Context & Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to establish a mutually beneficial relationship between WM and Ngāti Kuku and to support positive outcomes for Ngāti Kuku and the Whareora Marae community. The intent is to record the resolution reached between the Parties concerning the Air Discharge Consent and to record mutual agreement that WM will cease their operations at the site at the completion of the 15-year term.

◈Principles
  • Engagement Principle 1: Recognition of Ngāti Kuku's mana and rohe.
    WM acknowledging the impacts and burden of the industry has had on iwi and that despite these intergenerational impacts Ngāti Kuku continues to uphold kaitiakitanga.
  • Engagement Principle 2: Tikanga and good faith
    Both agree to act reasonably and in good faith in relation to entering this agreement.
  • Engagement Principle 3: Tauranga Moana and Whareroa Marae
  • Engagement Principle 4: Environmental, Economic, and Community Outcomes
✦Outcomes & Commitments
  • Investment Commitment: While WM operate the Site, WM will pay Ngāti Kuku $XXXXX per annum by way of quarterly instillments.
  • Technical Support and Knowledge: Upon request, WM will make available relevant members of its internal team to provide technical advice or information relating to environmental management, sustainability, waste and recycling systems, or resource consent processes, whether or not those matters relate directly to the site.
  • Air Discharge Consent: WM agrees to amend its consent application to seek a new term of 15 years instead of 30 years.
  • Resource Consent Support: Ngāti Kuku agrees to update its position to reflect support for the Air Discharge Consent. If requested by WM, Ngāti Kuku will attend hearings, file evidence in support and take all steps to assist WM in obtaining the Air Discharge Consent and also any other consents necessary to continue operation at the Site.
⇄Engagement Structure
Joint Partnership Committee: Governance structure to oversee this Agreement.
↻Review & Termination
  • The term of this Agreement will run for a term aligned with the Air Discharge Consent.
